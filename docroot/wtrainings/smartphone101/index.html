Smartphone 101

Welcome

In this course we’ll discuss the technology that makes a smartphone… well, smart! The more you understand smartphones, the more confident you’ll be when demonstrating features, comparing products, and providing the expert advice that can lead to a sale.

Selling Tip: Show your customers what smartphones can do. Demonstrate apps, the camera, or play a game or video to help customers imagine how a smartphone would make a difference in their life.

Processor

The processor is the “heart” of the smartphone. The processor directly controls functions like battery life, touchscreen responsiveness, camera shutter speed, access to apps and files, speed in executing commands, gaming graphics and smooth video playback.

Operating Systems and Apps

Different manufacturers, devices, features and operating systems (OS) mean your customers have a lot to consider when choosing a smartphone. Understanding the different operating systems will give you confidence in helping your customers.

Apple® iOS
AndroidTM
Windows Mobile®
BlackBerry®
Firefox®
  • Apple® iOS

    iOS is Apple's mobile operating system and it is available on the iPhone. Apps and content can be downloaded from the Apple App Store.

    The Apple logo and App Store badge are trademarks of Apple Inc.

  • Android

    Android is a mobile operating system developed by Google. It is used by many smartphone manufacturers who customize their user interface, so it’s a good idea to get familiar with the various devices in your store. With Android, apps and content can be downloaded from the Google Play store.

  • Windows Mobile

    Microsoft’s Windows Mobile operating system features a “live tiles” interface and apps, music, and games can be downloaded from the Windows Phone Store. There are several manufacturers of the Windows Phone.

  • BlackBerry®

    BlackBerry manufacturers phones with their own OS, including the BlackBerry 10 OS. Apps and other content are available through Blackberry World.

  • Firefox®

    This new mobile OS is available in some countries. Mozilla has developed the Firefox OS for smartphones and several hardware partners to bring devices to consumers. Apps are available through the Firefox Marketplace or the adaptive app discovery engine.

Selling Tip: Many customers want to be sure the smartphone they purchase will support their favorite apps. By teaching them how to search for apps they will become more confident when choosing the right smartphone.

Connect Everywhere

The right smartphone can connect customers to cellular networks like 4G LTE, location services, Wi-Fi hotspots and Bluetooth devices. Some smartphones can even create their own hotspot so that other devices can share an Internet connection.

Cellular Networks: 2G, 3G and 4G

The “G” stands for the “generation” of the mobile network. A higher number before the ‘G’ means more power to send and receive more data.

3G and 4G LTE are the latest generations, but not all smartphones can handle the increased bandwidth so it’s important to know how to identify a device that can support these advanced networks.

  • 2G: 2nd generation cellular technology

    2G was designed for voice calling and text messaging. Data speeds are pretty low.

    Qualcomm® invented cdmaOne – the technology that played an important role in the transition from 1G (analog) to 2G (digital) networks. Qualcomm’s integration of CDMA into devices also helped lay the foundation for supporting wireless data on mobile devices

  • 3G: 3rd generation cellular technology

    This generation has revolutionized mobile phones and has wide bandwidth and fast speeds.

    Qualcomm’s integration of 3G wireless into mobile processors helped drive the transition from handsets to smartphones and enabled internet access on mobile devices like laptops and tablets.

  • 4G: 4th generation cellular technology

    4G offers even MORE bandwidth than 3G and can offer speeds comparable to home DSL/cable.

    Qualcomm was the first to integrate 3G and 4G/LTE with the processor to enable a compelling smartphone experience and truly global broadband access, virtually anywhere.

Other Ways to Connect

There are many other ways to connect with a smartphone.

Wi-Fi
GPS and GLONASS
Bluetooth
USB®
HDMI®

  • Wi-Fi allows internet access through a wireless network access point. While the signal has a limited range, Wi-Fi can provide an extremely fast wireless connection.

  • GPS and GLONASS technologies access global navigation satellite systems to provide location and direction information. This allows real-time driving directions, location services like traffic and weather updates and fitness apps to measure time and distance.

  • Bluetooth is a wireless technology used for connecting close-range devices like headphones, keyboards, and hands-free headsets.

  • USB® is a wired technology most smartphones use to charge and transfer data.

  • HDMI is an interface for transferring uncompressed data. That means you can connect your compatible smartphone to an HDTV to watch content in high resolution.

Display

A gorgeous touchscreen is the one of the wow-factors of a smartphone. A screen that is bright and sharp is essential for reading email, and web pages. Color and contrast will show off the detail for graphics, photos and video.

There are different elements that affect the visual quality - like display technology (LCD, OLED), resolution, pixel concentration and color range. Get familiar with different devices so you can see how they perform differently.

Battery

All the speed and power in the world is nothing if your phone is dies in the middle of an important call and you’re nowhere near a charger.

Battery life can range anywhere from a few hours to several hours, depending on how your customer is using their device. Be sure to talk to customers about how important long battery life is to them.

Selling Tip: Qualcomm® Snapdragon™ processors are designed to improve battery efficiency by using power only when needed. Learn more about in the Snapdragon Processors course.

Camera

Smartphones have become a convenient replacement for point-and-shoot cameras. With apps like Instagram, Facebook and Vine, a high quality camera is something your customers expect.

A smartphone’s ability to deliver great images is determined by several things including Image sensor and processor.

  • Processor: The processor determines the speed and performance of your camera. Fast processors can capture multiple shots in a second!
  • Image Sensor: The image sensor is the digital version of film. A good sensor can capture ge, fine detail and less noise.
