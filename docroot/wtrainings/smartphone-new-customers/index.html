Smartphone for New Customers

Welcome

People are stepping from feature phones to smartphones faster than ever. The convenience and features of smartphones have changed how we interact with people and world around us.

This module will teach you how to help a customer choose their first smartphone – even if you don’t own one yourself.

Get to Know Them

If your customer is browsing smartphones, they're probably eager to know more. Your job is to understand their needs, answer questions, and show them how smartphone can make their life better.

Selling Tip: A great place to start with a customer is by asking, “What are you looking to do with your smartphone?” This is a great way to understand what features and applications are important to them.

The Basics

Almost everyone wants to be connected to friends, family and colleagues. Start by showing them the basic features:

  • Touch screen
  • Voice calls
  • Texting and conversation history
  • Voice to text

Selling Tip: First time smartphone shoppers tend to be overwhelmed. Start with the basics to help them become confident.

Web Access

A smartphone puts the internet your pocket. Show them how they can use the web just like they do at home:

  • Search for information
  • Send and read email
  • Shopping
  • Check news and weather

GPS & Maps

Smartphones allow you to take the power of GPS with you everywhere you go. Show them how to:

  • Pinpoint your current location
  • Find a nearby businesses
  • Get driving directions
  • Search for a map

Apps

Apps available for almost anything you can imagine. But the great thing about apps is it really customizes the phone to be personal. Ask them what their interests are and show them apps that they might like:

  • Social Networking (Facebook, Twitter)
  • Music (Pandora, Spotify)
  • Photo Sharing (Instagram)
  • Video & Movies (YouTube, Netflix)

The Camera

Smartphones have amazing cameras that can be just as good as the point-and-shoot you already own. Show them the camera and how they can:

  • Capture photos and video
  • Share through messaging, email, or social media
  • Edit with special effects

Put It All Together

A smartphone helps you connect, stay informed, and be entertained. Customizing your smartphone with apps makes your device personal providing you exactly the functionality you need.

Busy Parent

Amy is in charge of coordinating her kids' schedules, is an active volunteer, and likes to stay in touch with social media.

Show Amy how easy it is to use a smartphone calendar, and how she might even be able to sync it up with her son's or other family members. The camera will be great for capturing photos from some of her volunteering activities, and she can share them right to websites or social media.

Business Owner

Carlos runs a small business and is looking for ways that a smartphone can help him be more productive.

Talk to him about how apps for his smartphone can help him do everything from track expenses to manage inventory to even act as a point-of-sale. He can update his website or write to his company’s blog directly from his smartphone when inspiration strikes, and updating his social media is a breeze.

College Student

Erin is heading to college, and already has some devices in mind to check out in your store. She knows she'll be doing a lot of texting and social networking, but she's excited about taking pictures and shooting video as well.

Talk to Erin about everything a smartphone can help her do - from researching and writing school assignments to managing her class schedule - and as a college student with limited living space, she might really enjoy the home-theater-in-your-hand a smartphone can provide.

Congratulations

You have completed this training on Smartphones 101. Remember, this customer is probably moving up from a feature phone and considering their first smartphone. They’ve certainly seen them around and might be a bit familiar with some smartphone features, but they’ll appreciate knowing more about the convenience and personalization a smartphone can provide.

Help them see how a smartphone can become a part of their lifestyle, putting the things that are most important to them – specific apps, photos and videos, entertainment and gaming, or productivity features – right at their fingertips. Pick up the devices in your store, demonstrate the features, and let your customers interact with them.

First time buyers might look around a lot, visit your store several times, and have a lot of questions. Be ready to listen, help, and recommend.
