A (Really) Brief Overview of Wi-Fi

Wi-Fi has given us all a lot of flexibility and freedom and the technology continues to improve.

The industry standard for Wi-Fi technology is called 802.11. Versions of 802.11 are specified with letters - like n, a, or g. Each generation builds on the last, and typically offers improvements in speed, bandwidth and performance at range. 802.11n was introduced in 2007, is in the vast majority of devices today – and is probably in the router and devices you have now.

The newest standard is 802.11ac (or 11ac). It makes gigabit-speed Wi-Fi a reality, providing a lot more bandwidth than ever before to move your media and applications faster and more smoothly – and this is where Qualcomm VIVE sets the standard.

802.11ac Is The Fastest Wi-Fi Ever

802.11ac was introduced in 2013, and is 3 times faster than previous 11n technology. It delivers greater performance by transmitting more data streams over wider radio channels. It does this in the 5GHz wireless band, which has more available spectrum than the 2.4GHz band (where most Wi-Fi products operate today). Real-world throughput depends on the number of antennas and other router specifications, but today’s 11ac networks can deliver 600-900 Mbps actual user speeds (in the 5GHz band).

The new generation of wireless networks and devices will use 11ac to entertain, educate, and inform. This is where VIVE brings digital media to life.

Cleaner at 5GHz

Most Wi-Fi devices use the 2.4GHz band of the radio spectrum. With billions of Wi-Fi products using that spectrum – as well as household items like microwaves, cordless phones, even baby-monitors – it's a crowded airspace with lots of interference.

VIVE technologies use the larger 5GHz swath of the wireless spectrum on 802.11ac, which offers more channels vs. 2.4 GHz to reduce interference. For example, 5GHz allows for 41 non-overlapping 20/40/80/160MHz channels compared to 2.4 GHz at 4 non-overlapping 20/40MHz channels. VIVE makes the best use of this extra capacity to maximize performance - particularly in crowded environment. If you think of 2.4GHz as water through a straw, VIVE technologies using 5GHz is a fire hose.

Plays Well With Others

To realize all the performance advantages of 802.11ac, both the router and the device - like your smartphone, tablet, or notebook - need to support 11ac. If you still have a device that operates on 802.11n, VIVE has them covered.

VIVE is backwards compatible to 11n, and is designed with an architecture that is more power and data efficient even for legacy devices.

Channel Agility Keeps Data Moving

Channel Agility is a great feature that helps mitigate interference in crowded environments. For example, if you live in an apartment or condo, you will most likely have neighbors using 11a or 11n networks. This can lead to interference with your 11ac network causing potential degradation to your WiFi experience.

With the Channel Agility feature found in Qualcomm VIVE 11ac, your router can smartly operate around this interference and deliver you better Wi-Fi performance. For example, VIVE 11ac normally transmits over the wider 80MHz channel, which is a big lane to send lots of traffic for faster Wi-Fi. However, when other Wi-Fi networks like (11a/11n) occupy part of this 80 MHz channel causing interference, Channel Agility feature enables your router to automatically step back to 40 or 20MHz transmissions to mitigate interference and keep traffic moving. When the interference is gone, it jumps back to 80MHz transmission, so you always have optimal performance.

This ability to switch between channels is what makes Channel Agility a must have feature to have in your router. Unlike some competiting 11ac solutions, which get stuck waiting for the full 80 MHz channel to open up or switch to operate in narrow channels that can lead to delays and even drops in the connection.

Channel Agility is continually inspecting your environment to detect interference and then works around it. This improves your Wi-Fi throughput and delivers greater performance in any environment.
