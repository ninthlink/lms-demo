How is your customer communicating?

The mobile phone is no longer just a phone—it's a comprehensive communication tool.

1G/Voice

  • Basic voice service
  • Analog Cellular

2G/Voice + Messaging

  • Designed for voice
  • Digital Cellular
  • Better coverage, capacity
  • Can access 1G networks

3G/Mobile Broadband

  • High-speed mobile data
  • Designed for voice + data
  • Web browsing, video, GPS, location-based services and social sharing.
  • Can access 2G, 1G networks

4G/Advanced Mobile Broadband

  • True mobile broadband
  • Designed for data
  • Faster mobile experiences
  • Web browsing, video, GPS, location-based services and social sharing.
  • Can access 3G, 2G, 1G networks

How to help your customer
upgrade to a 3G smartphone

Show them that it is easy to use

Letting them try the device will make them feel confident and ease their worries.

Show them the useful new experiences they will get

Show them helpful apps that will provide useful information and entertainment.

Show them how affordable they can be.

Show them how they can get a 3G smartphone for about the price of a basic phone.

Add a 3G data plan

A 3G data plan makes a smartphone, well smart. If your customer has concerns, remind them that:

  • A 3G data plan often costs the same as a 2G plan1.
  • Apps and settings can help track, monitor data
  • Use Wi-Fi at home and 3G on-the-go for convenient connectivity options.

On the sales floor

A few ways to let them try the features

  • Let them hold and try the device.
  • Map your location.
  • Test a text or email.
  • Show them how to navigate.
  • Take a photo.
  • Show them helpful apps.
  • Watch a video.

Overcoming Objections

3G devices are too expensive.

Show them how there is a wide variety of 3G smartphones, and they can find one to fit their budget.

I like my 2G device.

With a 3G connection, you can still get 2G coverage in addition to 3G data capabilities.

My Wi-Fi connection is adequate.

3G is kind of like “Wi-Fi everywhere,” meaning it provides Internet access via the same radio towers that provide voice service to your mobile phone. 3G lets you have the freedom to go almost anywhere and still have a connection. (FYI: 4G is the same thing, just faster.)
